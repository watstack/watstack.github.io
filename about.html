Know when it's safe to breastfeed after drinking alcohol

What is FeedSafe?

FeedSafe is a free tool designed for breastfeeding mothers who want to enjoy an occasional drink without worry. It calculates how long you need to wait before breastfeeding or pumping for storage, based on what you drank and your body weight.

The app is built on guidance from the CDC, LactMed (NIH), and the Academy of Breastfeeding Medicine. It runs entirely in your browser — no account needed, no data collected.

How does the timer work?

Alcohol passes into breast milk at roughly the same concentration as your blood. As your body metabolises the alcohol, it clears from your milk at the same rate. The timer estimates when your blood alcohol level returns to zero based on:

  • The number of standard drinks consumed
  • The time you finished drinking
  • Your body weight (heavier people metabolise alcohol faster)

A general rule of thumb is to wait approximately 2 hours per standard drink. FeedSafe adjusts this based on your weight for a more personalised estimate.

Frequently asked questions

Does pumping and dumping remove alcohol faster?
No. Pumping does not speed up alcohol clearance. Alcohol leaves your milk as your blood alcohol level drops — the only thing that helps is time. You should only pump for comfort if you feel engorged, and discard that milk.
What counts as one standard drink?
In Australia, one standard drink contains 10 grams of pure alcohol. This is roughly equivalent to a 285ml glass of mid-strength beer, a 100ml glass of wine, or a 30ml shot of spirits. In the US, a standard drink contains 14 grams of alcohol.
Is it safe to drink at all while breastfeeding?
Most health authorities, including the CDC and Australian Breastfeeding Association, say that occasional moderate drinking is acceptable while breastfeeding, provided you wait for the alcohol to clear before feeding. The safest option is always to avoid alcohol, but timing feeds correctly minimises any risk.
Can I plan ahead for a night out?
Yes. FeedSafe includes a planning mode. Tap the time on any drink to set it to a future time — the app will calculate when you'll be clear so you can plan feeds or have expressed milk ready.
Does FeedSafe store my data?
Your drink log and weight are saved locally on your device using your browser's storage. Nothing is sent to any server. Clearing your browser data will reset the app.
How accurate is the timer?
The timer provides an estimate based on average metabolism rates. Individual factors like food intake, fatigue, medications, and liver health can affect how quickly you process alcohol. When in doubt, wait longer or consult your healthcare provider.
